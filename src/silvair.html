Silvair Platform

Lighting control platform based on the globally interoperable Bluetooth mesh standard.

My role

As one of the Senior UX designers, I contributed to the development of the Silvair Platform a tool for designing, configuring, and managing lighting networks based on Bluetooth Mesh technology. The platform enables the creation of modern lighting systems in commercial buildings, ensuring reliable operation, flexible deployments and access to advanced data analytics.

Web App – Planning Stage key flows

  • Create account & Start project: Smooth onboarding. Users set up a project workspace and manage team roles from the Collaborate section.
  • Set Floor Plans: Upload floor plans and create zones using a visual canvas for intuitive room organization.
  • Define Lighting Zones & Light scenarios: Assign zones, select pre‑built lighting control profiles (like daylight harvesting), and tweak parameters.

Mobile App – On‑Site commissioning

  • Device Discovery & Provisioning: Scan for mesh-capable luminaires, sensors, and switches, automatically matching them to planned zones.
  • Add Devices to Network: Easily add devices to the mesh using proximity-based lists for quick configuration.
  • Calibration & Testing: Calibrate daylight sensors, test triggers, and validate installations in real-time.
  • Review & Optimize: Compare real-world performance to plans, adjust parameters, and reassign devices if needed.

Our UX process began with concept sketches reaserch and collaborative workshops aimed at aligning the team around user needs and business goals. We facilitated structured design sessions where we explore early ideas and uncover pain points.

Next, we created initial user flows and low-fidelity sketches to visualize how users would interact with the system across different scenarios. These early artifacts helped us map out key screens and navigation patterns, ensuring that the overall structure supported intuitive and efficient workflows. At this stage, we also prepare prototypes and conducted user testing to validate our assumptions.

During the design handoff, we delivered detailed user flows and provided developers with high-fidelity mockups and specifications. We ensured that each interaction and screen state was clearly documented to support accurate implementation. Finally, we collaborated closely with the development team, iterating on the implementation until the commissioning app was ready for production release.
